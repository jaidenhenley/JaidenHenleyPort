CoastCast

Know before you go. Real beach conditions, scored and ready.

Overview

Know before you go. Real beach conditions, scored and ready.

CoastCast pulls weather, water temperature, wave data, crowd predictions, and water quality advisories into a single scored recommendation for 54 Michigan beaches. Instead of checking three different apps and guessing, you open CoastCast and know if the beach is worth the drive.

Highlights

  • Conditions scored across temperature, wind, UV, water quality, and crowd levels
  • CoreML crowd prediction trained on real Michigan visitation data
  • E. coli and water quality advisories from the EPA Water Quality Portal
  • Background notifications for favorited beaches with real conditions fetched at alert time
  • Siri integration via AppIntents for hands-free condition checks
  • FastAPI backend on Render handling buoy data, holiday detection, and parallel fetches
  • 54 Michigan beaches across all four Great Lakes and inland lakes

Tech Stack

  • SwiftUI
  • WeatherKit
  • CoreML
  • AppIntents
  • BackgroundTasks
  • FastAPI (Python)
  • EPA Water Quality Portal

Challenges & Solutions

01

Scattered Data, No Single Source

Challenge: Michigan beach conditions live across WeatherKit, NOAA buoys, DNR advisories, and EPA water quality portals. None of them talk to each other and none of them tell you whether the beach is actually worth going to.

Solution: Built a FastAPI backend on Render (MichiganWaterAPI) that pulls buoy data, weather, and holiday flags in parallel using asyncio.gather. On the iOS side, BeachScoringService combines all of it into a single weighted score per beach.

Result: One number that actually means something, backed by real data from multiple authoritative sources.

02

Matching Water Quality Advisories to Beaches

Challenge: The EPA Water Quality Portal doesn't map cleanly to a list of named beaches. Station coordinates exist but there's no direct lookup by beach name.

Solution: Matched EPA monitoring stations to CoastCast beaches using Haversine distance calculations, landing clean matches for 34 of 54 beaches. Unmatched beaches fall back gracefully without breaking the scoring pipeline.

Result: E. coli and water quality data surfaces automatically for covered beaches with no manual maintenance required.

03

Predicting Crowds Without Real-Time Data

Challenge: Crowd levels at beaches aren't tracked in real time. There's no API for it. But crowd size is one of the biggest factors in whether a beach visit is actually good.

Solution: Trained a GradientBoostingClassifier on IRMA Michigan visitation data combined with Open-Meteo historical weather. The model uses 12 features including cyclical month encodings (month_sin/month_cos). Exported to CoreML and runs fully on-device via MLDictionaryFeatureProvider.

Result: ~90% accuracy crowd predictions with zero network dependency at inference time.

What I Built

  • BeachScoringService with weighted scoring across temperature, wind, precipitation, UV, crowd, favorites, proximity, and tag match
  • FastAPI backend handling buoy data, holiday flags, and parallel API fetches via asyncio.gather
  • CoreML training pipeline in Python: data collection, feature engineering, GradientBoostingClassifier, coremltools export
  • On-device crowd inference via MLDictionaryFeatureProvider
  • EPA Water Quality Portal integration with Haversine-based station matching
  • LocationManager for proximity-aware scoring and sorting
  • Background notification system via BGAppRefreshTask with real-time condition fetching at alert time
  • Siri integration via AppIntents for hands-free beach condition queries
  • Single NavigationStack architecture driven by AppRoute enum and NavigationManager singleton

Next Iterations

The scoring is solid but the UI doesn't fully show it yet. I want to surface multiple reasons a beach scored the way it did, not just the primary one. Water quality coverage is at 34 of 54 beaches and I want to close that gap. On the notification side, content assertions and background refresh behavior still need real device testing. After that, App Store submission.

Outcome

CoastCast started as a question: why is it so hard to know if a beach is actually worth going to? Weather apps don't know the water temperature. Water temperature sites don't know the crowd. Nobody tells you about the E. coli advisory. I built CoastCast to answer all of it in one place, with a score you can actually trust. Fifty-four beaches, real data, one recommendation.
