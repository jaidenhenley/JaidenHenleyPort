QuickStudy

Scan notes and PDFs into flashcards—fast.

QuickStudy takes the friction out of making study materials. Import a scan or PDF, review what the AI generates, approve the cards worth keeping, then jump straight into flashcard practice or a multiple-choice quiz.

Highlights

  • Approve/edit cards before saving — no noise in your deck

Challenges & Solutions

01

OCR Messiness

Challenge: Scanned notes produce inconsistent OCR output — stray characters, broken words, merged lines.

Solution: Added a cleanup pass on extracted text before handing it to the model.

Result: Cleaner flashcard generation with significantly less manual correction.

02

AI Availability & Failures

Challenge: On-device Foundation Models aren't always available — older devices, low memory, or generation errors.

Solution: Built a deterministic fallback that splits text into card-sized chunks when AI isn't available, plus clear error UI so users always know what happened.

Result: Reliable card generation behavior across all supported devices.

03

Noisy Generated Cards

Challenge: Not every generated flashcard is worth studying — filler sentences, repeated facts, or low-value cards end up in the set.

Solution: Added an approval step where users toggle cards on or off before committing to a deck.

Result: Users only practice the cards they've deliberately chosen to keep.

Key Engineering Details

Technical decisions that shaped the final product.

On-Device AI with Fallback

Used Apple's Foundation Models framework to generate flashcard question/answer pairs directly on-device — no server, no data leaving the phone. When the model isn't available (older hardware or low memory), a deterministic splitter breaks the source text into card-sized chunks so generation always succeeds.

OCR Cleanup Pipeline

Raw VisionKit output often contains stray characters, broken hyphenation, and merged words. Before sending text to the model, I run a cleanup pass that normalizes whitespace, removes recognition artifacts, and re-joins hyphenated words — improving card quality without manual editing.

Approval-First Deck Building

Every generated card goes into a review list before it can be studied. Users toggle cards on or off, and only approved cards are written to the saved deck. This keeps study sessions high-signal and gives users control over what the AI produced.

Local-First Persistence

All decks, approved cards, and quiz state are stored locally. No account, no sync, no network dependency. Saved sets persist between sessions and can be reloaded for follow-up practice without re-importing the source material.
