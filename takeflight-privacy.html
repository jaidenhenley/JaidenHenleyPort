Take Flight

Privacy Policy

Take Flight is built with your privacy in mind. The developer does not collect or store any personal data.

No Data Collected by the Developer

Take Flight does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal data with the developer. There are no user accounts, no sign-in requirements, and no analytics or tracking of any kind. The app's privacy label reflects this: no data types are collected or linked to your identity by the developer.

Game Center

Take Flight integrates Apple Game Center for leaderboards and achievements. When you use Game Center features, Apple collects and manages your Game Center profile, scores, and achievement data in accordance with Apple's Privacy Policy. The developer has no access to your Game Center data beyond what Apple surfaces through public leaderboard APIs.

Game Center participation is optional. You can decline Game Center sign-in when prompted, or sign out in iOS Settings at any time.

Local Storage & Data Retention

Take Flight stores game progress, high scores, and settings locally on your device using SwiftData and standard iOS storage mechanisms. Nothing is synced to external servers by the developer. Because no data is collected by the app itself, there is no server-side retention period.

All locally stored content is retained on your device until you delete it or uninstall Take Flight. Uninstalling the app permanently removes all associated local data.

App Permissions

Take Flight may request the following iOS permission:

  • Game Center — used for leaderboards and achievements. Managed entirely by Apple.

Permissions are requested only when you initiate the relevant action. You can revoke Game Center access at any time in iOS Settings.

No Third-Party SDKs

Take Flight contains no third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking frameworks. There are no external dependencies beyond Apple's own frameworks (SpriteKit, SwiftUI, SwiftData, Game Center, AVFoundation) that could introduce data collection or sharing by a third party.

Deleting Your Data

Because all game data is stored locally on your device, you are always in full control of it. To remove all data associated with Take Flight, uninstall the app from your device. This permanently deletes all locally stored content.

For Game Center data (scores, achievements), manage or delete your Game Center profile through Apple's Settings or by contacting Apple directly.

There is no developer-side account to close and no server-side data to request deletion of — none exists.

Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights regarding your personal data under applicable law (including the EU General Data Protection Regulation, UK GDPR, and California Consumer Privacy Act). Because Take Flight does not collect, process, or store any personal data on external systems, there is no personal data held by the developer to access, correct, export, or erase.

If you have any questions or wish to exercise any rights, you can contact the developer directly at jaidenhenleydev@gmail.com.

Data controller: Jaiden Henley — jaidenhenleydev@gmail.com

Children's Privacy

Take Flight is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or under 16 in applicable EU member states). The app does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. Because no data is collected from any user, no data is collected from minors.

Apple's Role

Take Flight is distributed through Apple TestFlight and the Apple App Store. Apple may collect certain device and transaction information as part of operating the App Store, TestFlight, and iOS platform. This collection is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy, not this policy. The Take Flight developer has no access to any data Apple collects.

If you use iCloud Backup, iOS will include your locally stored Take Flight data in that backup. This is managed entirely by Apple and iOS — the developer has no access to backed-up content.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated date. Continued use of the app after any change constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
